About me

Hello again! Paulina here.

I wasn’t always a UX/UI Designer but I did a lot of designing. In my previous life (as a teacher) - I loved designing educational materials that made learning easier for my students. Every last detail about my day was thought through from the colors and fonts I used in my charts to my word choice because it was all part of conveying my messages effectively. Teaching was a constant assessment of what my students needed to hear, see or experience to help me help their learning process.

After about a decade in the field, maybe because I love adventure, I dropped everything, moved to NY and became curious about what else I could offer the world. Along the way I met a couple of UX Designers and fell in love with the design process because I realized it incorporated a lot of the things I enjoyed doing as a teacher.

I decided to embark on a yearlong mentorship to learn about user experience design and frontend development. The thing I enjoyed learning about the most was how to make digital products more accessible for users of all different backgrounds since I consider myself an advocate for diversity and minority groups. I’ve always been motivated by helping people and I like what I do now because it’s a way for me to continue using the skills I developed to help people in innovative ways.

I’m interested in using design as a tool to help people help others or help themselves in positive ways.

I invite you to check out my work and connect with me to see how we could work together!
